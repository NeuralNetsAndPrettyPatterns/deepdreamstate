Summary

A luxury cruise ship called the Chthonic welcomes its first passengers—a bachelorette party led by bride Emma. The ship's staff, particularly the charismatic cruise director Olivia and Captain William, orchestrate an immersive experience designed to make guests "feel" something transcendent. But beneath the marketed wellness offerings and games of chance, there's an undercurrent of control: the staff move with ritualistic precision, guests report strange sensations, and the ship itself seems to pulse with an older, darker purpose.

As the bachelorette party settles in, they encounter the ship's carefully curated activities—massages, gambling, and nightly gatherings around a ceremonial bell. Emma senses something off in the dynamic, while her friends dismiss the strange architecture and staff behavior as avant-garde immersion theater. Meanwhile, crew members like Mairead (the spa manager) and Fion (the chief engineer) navigate their own precarious positions, bound by debt and survival to the corporate machine that runs the vessel. Selene, the purser, seems to know more than she reveals.

The episode culminates as crew members begin deliberately steering specific passengers toward the upper decks and restricted areas, setting in motion events that will reshape the cruise. Emma's skepticism wavers when she encounters crew talking in coded language about "offerings" and "binding." The first hint emerges that this cruise is not merely entertainment but part of something far older—a convergence of ancient ritual and modern exploitation.
